  • Hi!
    I'm Mila Chadayammuri

    I am a PhD student in Astronomy at Yale University, currently on a Pre-Doctoral Fellowship at the Chandra X-Ray Center in Cambridge, MA. I run and analyse simulations of galaxy clusters to understand how the intra-cluster gas is affected by the activity of supermassive black holes and by collisions with other clusters. Both these processes leave signatures in how we see galaxy clusters with X-ray telescopes like Chandra, and understanding them is key to correctly measuring masses of galaxy clusters and testing different models of cosmology, i.e. what the Universe is made up of.

Our team, assembled on the spot, won the Fidelity Data Visualisation Prize at the 2019 Reality, Virtually Hackathon at the MIT Media Lab! The app is now available for download from our GitHub, so anyone with a VR headset can view it. A YouTube demo is available here.

At the 2019 Cambridge Science Festival, I hosted a talk at Harvard's Immersive Visualisation Center sharing high-resolution observations and simulations of the Universe on the very largest scales. The event was sold out, and I can't wait to do it again this coming year!

I joined the Astronomy on Tap community in New Haven, and am now part of the organising team in Boston. Together, we host free events at bars and restaurants around the city, with talks from real astronomers, games, and space mission swag to get people of all ages and background pumped about space.
